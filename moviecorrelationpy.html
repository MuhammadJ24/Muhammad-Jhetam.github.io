Movie Industry Correlation
in Python using Pandas

In this project movie industry data between the years 1980 and 2020
was cleaned, prepared and the analysed for correlations between fields using the python libraries: Pandas, matplotlib, seaborn and numpy.

Introduction

The Movie dataset used in this project was obtained on Kaggle.com. The actual dataset that was used can be found here, Movie Industry Dataset.

The code base for this project can also be found in my Github Repository.

Data Cleaning


Checking/Removing Null entries

In the case of this project, movies that had Null entries were not considered to ensure an accurate correlation analysis between them. Before removing these rows of data we first need to check if they even exist. The percentage of Null entries in each column of our initial dataset is shown below.


These Null entries were then removed from the dataset. After which we run check to see if the removal was indeed successful.


Standardization of Data

It was observed that the budget,gross and votes columns in our datset were formatted as the float64 data type with a single decimal place of .0 at the end of each entry. However, none of these columns contained a float64 value that had a decimal other than 0, which means the decimals are not needed. Therefore, these columns were converted into the int64 to drop the unnecessary decimals and save storage space(however small the difference, it is a good practise).


Checking for and removing Duplicates

The dataset was then checked for duplicate entries, of which there were none.

Budget vs. Gross Earnings

The budget to make a movie and the gross earnings of that movie have a strong correlation. That was the first hypothesis analysed in this project. To do this, the budget and gross fields of the dataset were plotted against each other.


Based of this graphic there seems to be a correlation between the movie's budget and its earnings. However, let us confirm this with a regression plot.


As can be seen there is clearly a fairly strong correlation between the movie's budget and its gross earnings. The higher the budget, the higher the earnings of the movie tends to be.

The correlation between a movie's budget and it gross earnings make sense, in that the a higher budget may be linked to movie having a better cast, equipment, special effects(if required), better filming locations and even better the creation of better scenes in the case of adventure, action and other movies related to those.

General Correlation

In this section, a correlation matrix was used to comapre all "numerical" type entries in the dataset against each other, to look for any correlations between them.


In the above table, there is a fairly strong correlation between the budget and gross of each movie, as seen earlier.

There is also a moderately strong correlation between the gross and votes of each movie. This is an unsuprising trend, the votes are an idication of a positive response from the general viewer. It makes sense that the more votes a movie receives from fans, the higher the earnings of the movie tends to be.

Interestingly, the correlation matrix shows that there is a moderately weak correlation between the fans votes and the rating score given by movie critics. This means that the rating score of a movie given by critics does not have a strong impact on whether or not fans vote for the movie, i.e, they think that its good.

The rating score given to movie also has a very weak correlation to the gross earnings of the movie, it can be said that the rating score of a movie has little, to no impact on its earnings

Final Remarks

This project required a variety of data cleaning and preparation techniques in python, before allowing us to perform an analysis on the movie industry, which yielded valuable insight on correlations between factors surrounding movies and their grossings.
The Budget and Viwer approval of a movie tend to be its biggest contributors to it being a high grossing movie.
It was also found that the Critic Rating Score does not have a strong influence on its grossings.
